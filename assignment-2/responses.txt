One way that data travels on the internet is:
- in bits. Information is processed into binary code, 1's and 0's, which
can be sent through electricity, light, or radio waves to the receiving
end.

The relationship between IP address and the DNS is:
- The DNS associates names of websites with the corresponding IP address,
 to help your device connect to the destination on the internet. IP addresses
 are the locations of devices and websites that DNS uses to send each device
 to the correct location upon request.

A cookie is:
- one or more pieces of information stored as text strings on your device
that your browser stores after visiting a page. Your browser stores them to
help make browsing better by allowing a server to recall any customized
information you had saved for that site.

The three basic parts of a URL are:
- protocol
- domain name
- path
